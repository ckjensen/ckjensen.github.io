Throughout the painting process, from making composition decisions through painting the final stroke, I keep in mind the inspiration for creating that painting. My blog shares techniques and what initially inspired certain paintings and show the original photograph, if one exists. One thing to notice is that I may or may not stay true to the photo. That's where inspiration translates into creativity.

Feel free to email me any comments and questions. I would love to hear what you think. Thanks for your interest!

Looking Up March 6, 2020
Fractals! The inspiration for this painting came to me by looking out into my backyard trees and seeing winter still holding. I found a photograph that I had saved a last spring in my "To Paint" folder with the leaves blossoming. Staring up through the leaves you notice the "randomness" of the branches within the composition. Are they really random? Each branch influences the other's growth setting up a pattern based on the tree type, wind, sunlight, etc. Taking this same concept out to anything in nature, they all grow and bend based on dependencies. I was discussing this randomness with my daughter and she pointed me to Fractals. It is all quite interesting and this same theory is applied in economics to test if data was manufactured or true.
