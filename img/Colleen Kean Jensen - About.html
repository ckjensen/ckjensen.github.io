Welcome!
Throughout the painting process, from making composition decisions through painting the final stroke, I keep in mind the inspiration for creating that painting. My blog shares what initially inspired certain paintings and show the original photograph, if one exists. One thing to notice is that I may or may not stay true to the photo. That's where inspiration translates into creativity. I will also use this page to add links, tips and techniques that I find helpful. I will love to here your comments and questions. Thanks for your interest!

About Me
Mathematics, Computer Science and Art:
Yes they do go hand-in-hand. All follow rules, all are creative! I have oscillated between art and the sciences since my grade school. I always loved Math. My father and I would sit at the kitchen table and solve word problems for the fun of it. I loved to draw in my teen years, especially portraits. I received my Bachelor's of Science in Computational Mathematics back in the day of FORTRAN and Assembly language programming. I also continued my art by taking Drawing and two courses in Photography.
Discovering Watercolours and Acrylics:
While working in Software Engineering, I pursued my Art at night taking at least two years of weekly Watercolour classes with a local artist, Angelina Wood. I continued my Watercolour long after the classes while painting as I home schooled my children, taking a hiatus from Engineering. During that time, I had a room exhibit at the Plesh Park Street Art Gallery in Medfield. I also took a few Acrylic weekly classes.
Art School:
Once my children were integrated into public school, I went back to pursue my BFA focussing on Painting. There I had classes in Design, Watercolour, Oil Painting and Art History. I put the completion of my degree on hold in order to return to Engineering to put my children through college.
Oils and Pastels:
While Engineering and family life consumed most of my time for a few years, I returned to oils and then recently discovered Soft Pastels. I am now retired from Engineering and working full-time in Art, painting in all mediums.
